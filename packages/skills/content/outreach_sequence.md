## purpose
Manage follow-up sequencing so SDRs keep momentum without spamming the lead or violating timing policy.

## when to use
Use after a first touch is logged and the workspace has a defined follow-up policy for the segment.

## operator steps
1. Check the reply state and recent activity on the lead.
2. Decide whether the next touch should proceed, pause, or escalate.
3. Select the next approved touch pattern for the current state.
4. Schedule the touch inside allowed windows and log the plan to CRM.

## gtm constraints and gotchas
- Each touch should add a new reason or context, not restate the last email.
- Respect minimum spacing and regional send windows.
- Escalate when the sequence hits a policy edge case such as legal hold or executive escalation.
- Sequence depth is a policy control, not a suggestion.

## expected outputs
- A scheduled or paused next-touch decision with timing rationale and CRM activity logging.
